Research Experiences

Reflection and Transmission of Borehole Stoneley Wave across Permeable Structures 2019 Dec. – 2020 Nov.

To prepare for the practical application and analysis of Stoneley wave data. A borehole model of stratified porous formation was constructed based on Biot theory. The reflection and transmission of Stoneley wave across permeable and fractured formation are studied using the finite difference method and effective wave number analytical method, which leads to the recent publication on Chinese Journal of Geophysics(In press).

Finite Difference Modelling in the Multiphysics Structures Including Poro-crack Media 2020 Dec. – 2021 Aug.

To honor the attenuation effects of the elastic wave propagations due to the existed pores and cracks of the subsurface, a finite difference method is developed to simulate propagation of the elastic wavefield in the multi-physical structures including elastic, poro-elastic and poro-crack media based on a squirt-flow model (Tang et al, 2011 and 2012). Calculation results of the FD method are validated against the analytical solutions. This novel time-domain finite difference method solving the poro-crack elastic wave equations has been largely finished and are under preparation.

Evaluation Method of Perforation Penetration Depth Based on Acoustic Logging 2020 Jul. – 2021 Oct.

Project Description:

Finding and analyzing the relationship between perforation penetration depth and waveform information (such as P wave arrival time delay) by establishing a borehole perforation model and using a 3D finite difference code to simulate the borehole array acoustic responses under different perforation parameters, thus the results would yield interpretation charts which relates waveform information to perforation penetration depth in different perforation arrangement for the field perforation penetration depth evaluation.

Main Responsibility:
